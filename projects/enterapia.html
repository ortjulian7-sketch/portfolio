Summary

Enterapia serves both individual patients — matched with a therapist through an intake questionnaire — and companies offering mental health benefits through a dedicated B2B page, trusted by organizations like BetPlay, ICETEX, TCS, and Carvajal. I joined an already-running product as part of a technical and design team, and over two-plus years worked on improving its UX, automating payments, integrating third-party APIs (including WhatsApp Business via Meta), optimizing internal workflows, and shipping new features as the platform evolved.

Description

Improving a live product, not building from zero.

Unlike TROOP, Enterapia was already a functioning platform when I joined — my work here was about iterative improvement as part of a larger team of developers and designers, not solo end-to-end ownership.

My contributions spanned several areas: reworking parts of the UX to make the product more intuitive, automating payment processes that were previously manual or fragile, integrating the WhatsApp Business API (via Meta) for patient communication, and generally optimizing workflows behind the scenes so the platform could keep adding features without accumulating technical debt.

It's the kind of ongoing, collaborative work most production SaaS products actually need — less about a single dramatic rebuild, more about steady, reliable improvement over time.

Problem, solution & results

The problem

A live platform with real users needed continuous UX improvements and more reliable payment processing, without disrupting the experience for therapists and patients already using it.

The solution

Incremental UX improvements alongside payment automation and calendar integrations, shipped as part of a coordinated team rather than as isolated changes — keeping the product stable while it grew.

The results

A platform that's been in continuous operation for over 2 years across three countries, with smoother payment flows and a steadily improving user experience.
